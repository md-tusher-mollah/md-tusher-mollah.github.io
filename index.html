Biography

Dr. Md. Tusher Mollah completed his Ph.D. in Manufacturing Engineering from the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), Denmark, with full funding from the Independent Research Fund Denmark (Danmarks Frie Forskningsfond). His doctoral research focused on Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) modelling and experimental analysis of Material Extrusion Additive Manufacturing (MEX-AM). He later continued at DTU as a Research Assistant and Postdoctoral Researcher, contributing to research on additive manufacturing, CFD modelling, and vacuum-assisted resin infusion processes for wind turbine blade manufacturing.

Prior to his Ph.D., he completed his B.Sc. (Hons.) in Mathematics in 2016 and M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics in 2018 from Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman Science and Technology University, Bangladesh, both with distinction. He has published more than 40 peer-reviewed journal and conference papers with over 900 citations, and his long-term research vision focuses on advancing computational engineering, additive manufacturing, and sustainable manufacturing technologies through high-fidelity numerical modelling, experimental validation, and international collaboration.
