Biography

Dr. Md. Tusher Mollah completed his Ph.D. in Manufacturing Engineering from the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), Denmark, with full funding from the Independent Research Fund Denmark (Danmarks Frie Forskningsfond). His doctoral research focused on Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) modelling and experimental analysis of Material Extrusion Additive Manufacturing (MEX-AM). He later continued at DTU as a Research Assistant and Postdoctoral Researcher, contributing to research on additive manufacturing, CFD modelling, and vacuum-assisted resin infusion processes for wind turbine blade manufacturing. Prior to his Ph.D., he completed his B.Sc. (Hons.) in Mathematics in 2016 and M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics in 2018 from Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman Science and Technology University, Bangladesh, both with distinction.

Dr. Mollah has extensive research experience in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), fluid mechanics and heat transfer, rheology, additive manufacturing, composite manufacturing, porous media flow modelling, and multiphysics simulation. His research contributions include material extrusion additive manufacturing, 3D concrete printing, multilayer deposition stability, reinforcement integration, corner precision in filament-based printing, and vacuum-assisted resin infusion processes for wind turbine blade manufacturing. He has published more than 40 peer-reviewed journal and conference papers in internationally recognized journals, with over 900 citations. His work has been published in leading journals including Advanced Science, Additive Manufacturing, Cement and Concrete Research, Composites Part B: Engineering, and additive-manufacturing and composites-related conference proceedings. He also received the Best Paper Award at the Solid Freeform Fabrication (SFF) Symposium 2021 in Texas, USA.

In addition to research, Dr. Mollah has strong experience in teaching, academic supervision, and interdisciplinary collaboration. He has taught undergraduate Mathematics and Engineering Mathematics courses in Bangladesh and Denmark and supervised multiple undergraduate and postgraduate research projects. He is proficient in several computational and engineering tools including FLOW-3D, OpenFOAM, ANSYS Fluent, MATLAB, FORTRAN, C/C++, SolidWorks, and Autodesk Fusion. His long-term research vision focuses on advancing computational engineering, additive manufacturing, and sustainable manufacturing technologies through high-fidelity numerical modelling, experimental validation, and international collaboration.
